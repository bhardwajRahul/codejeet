When preparing for technical interviews at top tech companies, understanding the specific patterns and expectations of each employer is crucial. Both Uber and Coupang are major players, but their interview landscapes, as reflected in their coding question profiles, differ significantly in scale and focus. This comparison analyzes the question volume, difficulty distribution, and core topics to help you strategize your preparation effectively.

## Question Volume and Difficulty

The most striking difference is the sheer volume of documented questions. Uber's profile, with **381 questions**, is vast and well-trodden, indicating a highly standardized and predictable interview process. The difficulty breakdown (54 Easy, 224 Medium, 103 Hard) shows a strong emphasis on Medium and Hard problems, suggesting interviews are designed to rigorously test problem-solving under pressure and the ability to handle complex algorithmic challenges.

In contrast, Coupang's catalog is much smaller at **53 questions**. The distribution (3 Easy, 36 Medium, 14 Hard) still skews heavily toward Medium and Hard problems, but the limited dataset means the interview process might feel less predictable. You may encounter a wider variety of original problems or deeper follow-ups on a smaller set of core concepts. The high percentage of Medium questions for both companies confirms that mastering this difficulty level is non-negotiable.

## Topic Overlap

The core technical topics for both companies are nearly identical, centering on fundamental data structures and algorithms. The primary topics listed—**Array, String, Hash Table, and Dynamic Programming**—form the essential toolkit for any software engineering interview.

- **Array and String** manipulation is foundational, often involving two-pointers, sliding window, or matrix traversal techniques.
- **Hash Table** usage for efficient lookups and frequency counting is ubiquitous.
- **Dynamic Programming** appears as a key advanced topic for both, indicating you must be prepared to solve optimization problems involving memoization or tabulation.

Given this high overlap, a strong foundation in these areas will serve you for interviews at either company. The main difference lies not in _what_ you study, but in the _depth and variety_ of practice you might need. Uber's large question pool allows for pattern recognition across many problems, while preparing for Coupang requires a deeper, more conceptual mastery of these topics to handle potentially novel problem formulations.

## Which to Prepare for First

Your preparation strategy should be guided by the breadth of coverage needed.

**Start with Uber's question list if your goal is broad, pattern-based preparation.** The enormous volume provides an extensive workout on the shared core topics. Solving a wide range of Uber's Medium and Hard problems will build a robust algorithmic intuition that is directly transferable to Coupang and many other firms. It ensures you've seen a high number of problem variations.

**You can effectively prepare for Coupang by mastering the shared fundamental topics deeply.** Since the question pool is smaller, focus on achieving complete fluency with Arrays, Hash Tables, Strings, and Dynamic Programming. Ensure you can derive solutions for novel problems that fit these patterns, rather than just recognizing specific question prompts. Practicing a curated set of Medium and Hard problems from a general study plan will be highly effective.

In practice, preparing for Uber inherently covers the technical foundation for Coupang. The reverse is less true due to the difference in volume. Therefore, building your core skills using a broad set of problems (like those from Uber's list) is the most efficient primary strategy. You can then tailor your final preparation by reviewing the specific, smaller set of reported questions for your target company.
